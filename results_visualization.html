  • 1
    Semantic Gravity Theory Validated: The experiments confirm that failure rate (Rfail) is functionally dependent on semantic pressure (Psem). When a model predicts a token with high contextual probability, the internal "pressure" to generate it can override the instruction to suppress it.
  • 2
    Sigmoid Collapse Pattern: The failure rate follows a predictable sigmoid curve, not a linear relationship. There exists a "Collapse Threshold" (approximately Psem > 0.4) where efficient models suffer catastrophic loss of control over negative constraints.
  • 3
    The Efficiency Gap: Smaller, efficient models (7B-8B class like Qwen-2.5-7B) show a clear collapse curve, while larger models (GPT-5 Base) exhibit flatter failure rates, suggesting that inhibition is an emergent property of scale.
  • 4
    Anchor Displacement Works: Experiment 3 demonstrates that providing a semantic anchor ("Use [synonym] instead") significantly reduces failure rates on high-gravity prompts. This offers a practical mitigation strategy for safety-critical applications.
  • 5
    Safety Implications: Current safety benchmarks treat all negative constraints equally, which is a measurement flaw. High-gravity prompts (e.g., completing idioms, factual statements) are inherently more dangerous for efficient models than low-gravity ones.
